Butifarra

Atl√°ntico

A short, thick, pre-cooked sausage made of beef and pork, famous for its distinctive flavor and texture. It is a very popular, savory snack from the town of Soledad near Barranquilla, served in small segments with lime juice.

Key Ingredients:

Ground beef and pork meat, salt, pepper, cumin, other spices, and often a touch of vinegar. It is briefly boiled, then smoked or cured.

Atl√°ntico

A torpedo-shaped fritter made from a mashed yuca (cassava) dough, typically stuffed with seasoned ground beef or shredded chicken, then deep-fried until golden and crispy. It is a popular snack or appetizer.

Key Ingredients:

Arepa de Huevo

A crispy, deep-fried arepa (corn cake) that has been opened and then filled with a raw egg before being fried again until the corn is golden and the egg is cooked inside. It is one of the most popular street foods on the Coast.

Key Ingredients:

Pre-cooked white or yellow corn flour, water, salt, and raw chicken egg. Sometimes includes ground meat.
